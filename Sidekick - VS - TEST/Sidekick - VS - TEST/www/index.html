Welcome to Arcade Sidekick

I'm a huge fan of retro games and with the resurgence of the arcades I found myself wanting to keep track of my scores on my favourite machines. Thus Arcade Sidekick was born. Keep track of the games you have played, how you rate them, your top score and what the community think. Challenge your friends and enemies and publish your achievements on social media!

FAQ

Why have you developed Sidekick?
  • Sidekick started off as a personal project to record my scores and the games I had and wanted to play. After developing it for some time i showed a few friends who also wanted to use it. The idea grew from there. I added more games, more artwork, ratings, leaderboards and before i knew it i had something that my friends were suggesting i make available to everyone on the app stores. This was a pretty scary thought, but it became my ambition to release an app and so here it is, and here you are reading about why i made this. I want the sidekick to encourage friendly competition between friends, to help keep the memory and nostalgia of the retro gaming scene alive and to help preserve these classic games so they are not forgotten. If you have any suggestions as to how i can make the sidekick better please let me know and contact me via email or twitter.
What games are listed?
  • We currently list the vast majority of games from Europes largest arcade - Arcade Club UK. They acquire new games regularly which we will aim to keep on top of and update as soon as possible. We're planning on continuing on the list of the worlds largest arcades to cater for as many locations as possible and will be adding more games regularly. If there is a game that you want including in the system then please get in touch via email or twitter and we'll do our best to make it available in the next update.
How do I request a game?
  • The easiest way to request a game is to press the request game button in the custom games section, but you can also email or tweet us your request. Tweeting of course gives the rest of the community the chance to vote on your suggestion, and the posts with higher votes are most likely to get into the next updates.
How do you classify a game?
  • Dont agree with where we've placed a game? Its hard to segment every game into 1 category but we've done our best. If you think a game belongs in a different category then please contact us, we want the classification to be as accurate as we can. Some games just dont fit any well known genre so we've popped them into miscellaneous. Below is the definition we have used for each category
  • Beat'em ups - or brawlers, hand to hand combat between the protagonist and an obscene amount of opponents, typically sidescrolling in 2D.
  • Fighting - combat between a limited number of characters (usually 2 or 4) where the fight continues until all but one combatant is defeated or the timer expires.
  • Light guns - primary element is aiming and shooting with a gun-shaped controller, where players shoot at multiple targets moving through a level or standing stationary.
  • Hack 'n' slash - real-time combat with hand-to-hand weapons as opposed to guns or fists.
  • Maze - playing field is a maze which players have to navigate round, avoiding obstacles or enemies.
  • Platformer - jumping and climbing between suspended platforms.
  • Puzzle - puzzle solving and problem solving skills, logic pattern recognition etc.
  • Racing - first or third person perspective where player takes part in racing competitions, whether it be by land, water, air or space vehicle.
  • Rhythm - music-themed video games that challenges a player's sense of rhythm. Movement or button presses dictated on screen that must be followed along to the beat of music.
  • Run 'n' Gun - subgenre of shooters occasionally with platform elements, primarily focused on the protagonist (mostly human) moving quickly on foot and firing with near constant gun fire.
  • Shooter - traditionally set in space. Fighting done by a single spacecraft against multiple enemies.
  • Sports - simulates the practice of sports.
  • Miscellaneous - anything i couldnt categorise into one of the other categories. If through time enough games appear in misc that could have their own category then a new category will be added.
Can I request a new feature?
  • Please do! If you have any ideas how we can make this application better serve the arcade gaming community let us know! The app was developed to meet my own personal needs when playing games at arcades, but if you have any further ideas then please email or tweet us and im happy to have a chat about them! My vision is that together we can all get the app to one that best serves the needs of arcade go-ers and retro gamers.
How often will you add new games?
  • We'll aim to add new games every couple of weeks and have regular updates to the system. Depending on whether we're going for new features or updating our catalogue you might see more or less being added.
Some game details are wrong
  • Oops. Sorry. With hundreds of games its possible mistakes have been made so please get in touch and we'll aim to update the incorrect information as soon as possible.
We want more gifs and bezels!
  • We love the game gifs and bezels. When time allows we will aim to add more images to the existing games. We hope one day to have a gif and bezel for every game, but that will be a difficult task. If youve got a gif or bezel of a game that youd like to share with us to assist in this goal that would be great.
What data is stored online?
  • When you create a username we store some data for you so that it can be retrieved if you move onto a different device and viewed by your friends. We will store what you have rated a game and your highest score.
What features are on the roadmap?
  • We have lots of ideas for the app going forward. Here are just a few.
  • More games
  • More artwork
  • Extra themes
  • Achievements
  • More game information
  • Where the games are available to play
  • News
  • Links to wiki, videos etc
  • We would really appreciate your feedback on these features and which ones youd like to see first. As mentioned before its also really important to us that we have the features that you want to see, so suggest a new feature to us and it can be added to the roadmap for the future.
Why do I need a username?
  • You don't need one, but we would like you to make one. A username identifies you on leaderboards and enables us to store your score/rating information.
If I've been storing scores without a username will I lose them all if I create an account?
  • No. When you create a username all your scores and ratings will remain. The ratings will be uploaded automatically and you will be able to upload your scores through the my scores section from the dashboard.
I've forgotten my username/email/secret key?
  • If you've forgotten any of your information then please follow the links in the help section of the setup screen. For username/secret key queries we will send you an email. If you've forgotten your email you'll receive a helpful hint. If you've forgotten everything, then contact us but there might not be anything we can do!
Will you use my email address with any third parties?
  • Absolutely not. The email address is purely for our purposes. If we do get approached by any arcade venues wanting your information we would of course seek your permission first. Everyone hates spam.
How do i leave feedback?
  • Please leave feedback and ideas to arcadesidekick@outlook.com or to our twitter account, there are handy links in setup to get in touch with us! Please help us drive the future development of the app and make it the app you want to see.
